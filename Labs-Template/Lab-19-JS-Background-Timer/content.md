## Color Specifications

### Initial Background Color
- **Color:** `#ffffff` (White)

### Color After 5 Seconds

### Color Cycle Sequence (for bonus task)
| Step | Color Code | Color Name |
|------|-----------|------------|
| 1 | `#ffffff` | White |
| 2 | `#e6f3ff` | Light Blue |
| 3 | `#fff3e0` | Light Orange |
| 4 | `#e8f5e9` | Light Green |
| 5 | `#fce4ec` | Light Pink |
| 6 | Back to `#ffffff` | White (restart cycle) |

### Cycle Interval
- Change color every **3 seconds**

## Page Content

### Article Title
**The Importance of Color in Web Design**

### Paragraph 1
Color is one of the most powerful tools in a web designer's toolkit. It can evoke emotions, guide attention, and create a memorable user experience. In India, colors hold deep cultural significance — saffron represents courage, white symbolizes peace, and green stands for prosperity. Understanding how to use color effectively is essential for every aspiring web developer at Mandsaur University.

### Paragraph 2
Studies show that users form an opinion about a website within 50 milliseconds, and color plays a major role in that first impression. A well-chosen color palette can increase brand recognition by up to 80 percent. For example, banking websites in India like SBI and HDFC use blue tones to convey trust and reliability, while e-commerce sites like Flipkart and Myntra use vibrant colors to create excitement and urgency.

### Paragraph 3
When designing websites, it is important to consider color accessibility. Approximately 8 percent of men and 0.5 percent of women worldwide experience some form of color vision deficiency. Using sufficient contrast ratios and not relying solely on color to convey information ensures that your website is usable by everyone. Tools like WebAIM Contrast Checker can help you verify your color choices meet accessibility standards.
