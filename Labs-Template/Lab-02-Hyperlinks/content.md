## Page Title
Networking Technologies at Mandsaur University

## Paragraph 1: Campus Connectivity
Students at Mandsaur University enjoy seamless connectivity across the campus thanks to modern networking infrastructure. The entire campus is covered by **Wi-Fi** access points, allowing students to connect their laptops and smartphones from classrooms, the library, and even the canteen. Inside the computer laboratories, all 120 computers are connected through a high-speed **LAN** (Local Area Network) using **Ethernet** cables, which provides faster and more reliable connections compared to wireless networks. The university also uses **Bluetooth** technology for short-range device pairing, such as connecting wireless keyboards and mice in the seminar halls.

## Paragraph 2: How the Internet Works
When a student at Mandsaur University types a website address into their browser, several networking technologies work behind the scenes. First, the **DNS** (Domain Name System) translates the human-readable domain name into an IP address that computers understand. The request then travels using the **HTTP** (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) to fetch the webpage from a remote server. All this communication follows the rules defined by **TCP/IP** (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol), which is the fundamental protocol suite that powers the internet. The university's network connects to the wider internet through a **WAN** (Wide Area Network) link provided by the Internet Service Provider.

## Paragraph 3: The Importance of Networking Knowledge
As BCA students, understanding these networking concepts is essential for building web applications. Knowing how **Wi-Fi** and **LAN** networks operate helps in troubleshooting connectivity issues during lab sessions. Understanding **HTTP** and **DNS** is crucial for web development — every time you deploy a website, these protocols are at work. The department regularly conducts workshops on networking fundamentals, where students learn to configure **Ethernet** connections, set up **Bluetooth** file sharing, and understand how **TCP/IP** enables communication between millions of devices across **WAN** networks worldwide.
