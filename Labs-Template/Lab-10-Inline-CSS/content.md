## Paragraph 1 — Diwali (Background Color: `#fff8e1`)

Diwali, also known as the **[bold + color: red]** Festival of Lights **[/bold + /color]**, is one of the most celebrated festivals in India. Families decorate their homes with **[underline + color: orange]** diyas **[/underline + /color]** (oil lamps) and create beautiful **[underline + color: orange]** rangoli **[/underline + /color]** patterns at the entrance of their houses. The festival holds deep spiritual significance as people perform **[italic + color: purple]** Lakshmi Puja **[/italic + /color]** to seek blessings of wealth and prosperity from Goddess Lakshmi. Children enjoy bursting firecrackers in the evening, while elders share sweets like kaju katli and gulab jamun with neighbours and relatives.

---

## Paragraph 2 — Holi (Background Color: `#e8f5e9`)

Holi, popularly called the **[bold + color: blue]** Festival of Colors **[/bold + /color]**, marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. People gather in streets and open grounds to smear each other with **[color: green]** gulal **[/color]** (coloured powder) and spray coloured water using pichkari (water guns). The night before Holi is celebrated as **[italic + underline]** Holika Dahan **[/italic + /underline]**, where bonfires are lit to symbolise the burning of evil. Families prepare thandai — a refreshing drink made with milk, almonds, and spices — and enjoy festive delicacies like gujiya and malpua together.

---

## Paragraph 3 — Navratri (Background Color: `#fce4ec`)

Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to the worship of **[bold + color: maroon]** Goddess Durga **[/bold + /color]** and her nine divine forms. The festival is celebrated with great enthusiasm across India, especially in Gujarat and West Bengal. In Gujarat, people perform the traditional **[italic + color: crimson]** Garba **[/italic + /color]** and **[italic + color: crimson]** Dandiya **[/italic + /color]** dances every night, dressed in vibrant chaniya cholis and kediyu. Devotees observe fasting during these nine days and wear a specific colour each day — from red and blue to green and yellow — to honour the different avatars of the Goddess.

---

## Paragraph 4 — Eid (Background Color: `#e3f2fd`)

**[bold + color: green]** Eid-ul-Fitr **[/bold + /color]** is a joyous festival that marks the end of the holy month of **[underline]** Ramadan **[/underline]**, during which Muslims observe fasting from dawn to dusk. On Eid morning, families wake up early, wear new clothes, and gather at mosques or Eidgahs to offer namaz (prayer). After the prayers, the celebration begins with warm hugs, greetings of "Eid Mubarak," and the sharing of delicious **[italic]** sheer khurma **[/italic]** — a traditional dessert made with vermicelli, milk, dates, and dry fruits. Children look forward to receiving Eidi (gift money) from elders, and people contribute to charity to help those in need.

---

## Paragraph 5 — Christmas (Background Color: `#f3e5f5`)

**[bold + color: red]** Christmas **[/bold + /color]** is celebrated with great joy across India, especially in states like Goa, Kerala, Meghalaya, and Mizoram. Churches are beautifully decorated, and families attend **[italic]** midnight mass **[/italic]** on Christmas Eve to offer prayers and sing hymns. Homes are adorned with Christmas trees, twinkling lights, and star decorations hanging from balconies. Children eagerly await gifts from Santa Claus, and families bake plum cakes, prepare roasted chicken, and share sweets with neighbours. The spirit of the season comes alive through **[underline + color: purple]** carol singing **[/underline + /color]** — groups of people go from door to door spreading festive cheer through songs.

## Inline CSS Quick Reference

Use the `style` attribute inside HTML tags to apply inline CSS. Examples:

```html
<!-- Change text color -->
<span style="color: red;">Festival of Lights</span>

<!-- Bold text -->
<span style="font-weight: bold;">Important Word</span>

<!-- Italic text -->
<span style="font-style: italic;">Lakshmi Puja</span>

<!-- Underline text -->
<span style="text-decoration: underline;">diyas</span>

<!-- Multiple styles combined -->
<span style="font-weight: bold; color: red;">Festival of Lights</span>
<span style="font-style: italic; color: purple;">Lakshmi Puja</span>
<span style="text-decoration: underline; color: orange;">rangoli</span>

<!-- Paragraph background color -->
<p style="background-color: #fff8e1; padding: 15px;">Your paragraph text here...</p>
```
